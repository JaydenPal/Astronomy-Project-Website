Part A

Question One

This planet orbits at a faster pace due to it being closer to the sun, with a distance of 0.91AU and Earth’s being 1AU. This planet’s orbital period is 317.07 days or 0.87 yrs., compared to earth’s orbital period of 365 days and 1 year. Its days (or rotation period) are also shorter than Earth’s by 3.4 hrs. This planet’s Perihelion and Aphelion have shorter distances than Earth’s. The Perihelion is 0.86AU, compared to Earth’s 0.984AU. While the Aphelion is 0.96AU, compared to Earth’s 1.016AU. This means our planet has a greater orbital eccentricity. When viewed by an observer, the Sun will appear closer than is would when observed on Earth (angular size 59º)

Question Three

The seasons would be weaker because the tilt of the axis is not as much as earth’s tilt of the axis. Seasons would still occur but the changes wouldn’t be as strong as earth.

Our planet would not have equal lengths of seasons compared to earth because our planet has a greater orbital eccentricity than Earth's, meaning the difference between our Perihelion and Aphelion is greater than the difference of Earth's.

Since this planet has a smaller tilt than Earth, the seasons would be weaker in comparison. The planet’s faster rotation period and shorter orbital period will cause the seasons to be shorter in length and go by quicker. Both North and South hemispheres will have similar weather conditions since the planet has a small tilt of 8 degrees.

    Question One


    How does the composition of your planet’s atmosphere compare to Earth’s atmosphere?

    Our planet has an evolved atmosphere that has a very similar composition of gasses to Earth's atmosphere. Our planet does have a denser atmosphere than Earth's, as well as a larger scale hieght. Our planets atmosphere is a secondary atmosphere created through volcanic outgassing. This would cause large amounts of carbon dioxide to be present in the atmosphere, but our planet’s surface being composed largely of water, dissolves the carbon dioxide into its oceans. Between the two gasses left, nitrogen has the highest concentration in our atmosphere followed by oxygen.

    Question Two


    Do you expect stronger or weaker warm fronts and cold fronts during seasonal changes? || Would you expect to find hurricanes on your planet?

    Our fronts would be stronger due to faster rotation periods (ties into Coriolis affect) as well as a higher average surface temperature than earth allowing for faster winds. We would expect to find hurricanes towards the ends of the tropical region and around that area. They wouldn’t be likely to form over the equator for the same reasons they don’t form over the equator very often on earth. We would expect them to be stronger due to our planets increased surface temperature which would allow for the warmer waters hurricanes need to form. We would expect them to last around the same time or longer as they could be stronger storms that have more energy to last longer from stronger winds and warmer waters than on earth.
